# OctoAcme — Project Management Processes Overview

OctoAcme’s project management processes offer a structured yet adaptable framework that optimizes cross-functional collaboration and ensures consistent delivery outcomes. The lifecycle is divided into key phases—Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective—each with clear deliverables and decision gates. Projects start with a one-pager outlining problems, objectives, stakeholders, and success metrics, followed by actionable backlogs, risk registers, timeline agreements, and upfront acceptance criteria. Iterative delivery is emphasized, favoring small, testable increments, and active management of dependencies and risks.

Core roles within OctoAcme’s framework include the Project Manager (PM), who coordinates schedules, risks, and communications, and the Product Manager (PdM), who defines outcomes and prioritizes the backlog. Developers are responsible for design, implementation, and code quality, working closely with QA specialists to validate feature acceptance and enforce quality standards. All roles proactively identify risks, plan mitigations, and engage in transparent communication, using consistent templates for backlogs, risks, and retrospectives.

Communication practices are carefully defined to support team alignment and transparency, featuring weekly PM/PdM syncs, twice-weekly team standups, and regular stakeholder briefings. Escalation paths for blockers are clearly set, starting within the team and extending to sponsors for critical issues. Routine reporting—such as status updates, risk tracking, and milestone reviews—utilizes dashboards and standardized templates, keeping all stakeholders informed and engaged.

Quality assurance is integral to every phase, incorporating both automated and manual testing, code reviews, security scans, and checklists for each stage. Definition of Done and acceptance criteria are established early, with frequent demos and reviews to validate progress. Milestones and incidents trigger retrospectives, where lessons learned become actionable improvements, reinforcing OctoAcme’s continuous improvement culture and enabling teams to execute projects with transparency and sustained quality.
